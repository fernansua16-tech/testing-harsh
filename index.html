What is Butter Chicken?

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a North Indian dish made with marinated chicken simmered in a buttery tomato sauce. The flavor is rich, mildly spiced, creamy, and perfect with basmati rice or warm naan.

The traditional version uses smoky cooked chicken, but a home kitchen version can be grilled, broiled, pan-seared, or simmered directly in the sauce. The important part is building flavor in layers: yogurt marinade, toasted spices, tomato, butter, and cream.

How the Chicken is Cooked

For the closest restaurant-style flavor, marinate the chicken first, then grill or broil it until lightly charred. If you are short on time, pan-sear the pieces and continue with the sauce. Either way, save the marinade because it becomes part of the curry base.

The sauce starts with onion, garlic, and ginger cooked in ghee. Tomato puree and spices are added next, followed by butter and cream at the end so the curry stays smooth and glossy.

Butter Chicken Ingredients

The spice list looks long, but the method is simple. Garam masala, turmeric, cardamom, chili powder, cinnamon, and cloves give the dish its deep aroma. Almond flour helps thicken the sauce without making it heavy.

Helpful ingredient notes

Use full-fat yogurt for a smoother marinade, tomato puree for a bright sauce, and unsalted butter so you can control the final seasoning. Fresh cilantro and a swirl of cream make the final bowl look finished.

Make Ahead and Storage

This curry tastes even better after resting because the spices have time to settle into the sauce. Cool it completely, refrigerate it in an airtight container, and gently reheat on the stove.

If you want to freeze it, freeze the chicken and tomato sauce before adding cream. Stir in the cream after reheating for the best texture.

Butter Chicken Recipe

A simplified, home-friendly Murgh Makhani with creamy tomato sauce and tender chicken.

Prep10 min
Cook30 min
Marinate2 hr
Serves4

Ingredients

  • 2 lb boneless chicken, bite-size pieces
  • 1.5 cups full-fat plain yogurt
  • 0.25 cup almond flour
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2.5 tsp garam masala
  • 0.5 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 14 oz tomato puree
  • 0.25 cup butter
  • 0.33 cup heavy cream

Instructions

  1. 1. Mix yogurt, almond flour, lemon juice, garlic, ginger, salt, sugar, and spices. Coat the chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours.
  2. 2. Grill, broil, or pan-sear the chicken until browned. Keep the extra marinade for the sauce.
  3. 3. Cook onion in ghee until golden. Add garlic and ginger, then add chicken, marinade, and tomato puree.
  4. 4. Simmer covered for 20 to 25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. 5. Stir in butter and cream. Season to taste and garnish with cilantro.

Serving idea: Pair with steamed basmati rice, naan, cucumber raita, and a small squeeze of lemon.
